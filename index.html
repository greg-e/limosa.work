The Problem

The way time and tool use are tracked in the field today takes more effort than it should and still leaves gaps. Paper timesheets depend on workers remembering to fill them out. Phone apps assume coverage, battery, and willingness. Vehicle GPS can show where a truck went but not what the worker actually did.

The result: payroll cleans up mistakes, supervisors chase down missing hours, and managers don't get a clear view until the week is over. Every downstream decision — billing, job costing, scheduling, estimating — starts on shaky ground.

The Truth of Work

Every record tells a story. The goal is to make it the right one.

Capture what truly happens on a job as crews move — time, movement, and tool proximity. Data is stored on the badge and uploaded in cellular batches, creating a timestamped activity stream that is free of edits or recollection bias.

  • Verified start/stop per worker and crew
  • Tool use and duration from proximity events
  • Site entry, exit, and movement patterns by property (using tax parcels as geofences)
  • Immutable activity stream you can take action from

This is the truth layer — the data you can pay from, report from, and lead from. When work is logged as it happens, every downstream decision starts on solid ground.

Reverse-Engineering

You can't improve what you can't see flow.

Once work is captured truthfully, the next question is why it flowed the way it did. Actual data lets you reverse-engineer how jobs really run — where time goes, where constraints live, and where assumptions fall short.

a. Reverse-Engineered Schedule

  • Planned vs. actual task durations per property and route
  • Setup, travel, and delay patterns across the day
  • Hidden bottlenecks and repeatable flow patterns

b. Reverse-Engineered Estimate

  • Convert badge data into cost per task and parcel
  • Ground estimating models in observed work, not guesses
  • Continuously refine future bids and plans from real production rates

Truth becomes improvement. The same data that powers payroll also teaches how to plan better — no extra steps, no separate system.

How It Works

Edge Capture — The badge is an autonomous node. It captures GPS, BLE proximity, and accelerometer events at the point of work, buffers locally, and uploads over cellular in 30-minute batches. If coverage is intermittent, nothing is lost.

Cloud Truth — Events are ingested, normalized, and bound to context: job site via geofence, equipment via tag registry. Truth intervals are written to an immutable store. Anomaly detection flags discrepancies for review.

Downstream — Verified data flows into the systems the business already uses: payroll, job costing, scheduling, billing. Every downstream record carries its origin — the operator, the timestamp, the badge attestation.
