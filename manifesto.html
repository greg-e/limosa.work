Manifesto

Truth in Action

Actual builds autonomous, field-first tools that turn the act of working into trustworthy evidence. We minimize cognitive load, maximize flow, and keep human agency at the center.

← Back to Home

Core Beliefs

1) Flow over friction

Flow-enhancing habits outperform mechanical scheduling. We optimize the whole, not local efficiencies.

2) Convivial tools

Technology should extend personal agency and enable interdependence—not extract attention or obedience.

3) Truth as utility

Evidence must be deterministic, tamper-evident, and portable. Telemetry becomes proof, not paperwork.

4) Minimal footprint

Store visit windows, not tracks. Use parcel polygons, not circles. Prefer pseudonymous identities by default.

Selected Lines

“Conviviality is individual freedom realized in personal interdependence.” — Ivan Illich, Tools for Conviviality
“Flow-enhancement practices are critical to delivery and often more effective than mechanical ones.” — From Critical Chain to Critical Flow
“Liberate access to things, the sharing of skills, and the creative resources of people.” — Ivan Illich, Deschooling Society

How This Shows Up in Actual

  • Autonomous badge: logs parcel entrances/exits and asset proximity; cellular uplink on cadence.
  • Evidence bundle: deterministic JSON + signature; single source of truth per site visit.
  • On-badge attestation: simple end-of-day signal to close or trigger a return visit.
  • Privacy by design: pseudonymous IDs; minimize raw tracks; keep HR mapping separate.
  • System-agnostic: adapters for payroll, FSM, ERP, and BI; no vendor lock-in.
